Odu Ara

The 16 Sacred Body Postures is an SGI-styled course that transforms the 16 Odu into posture, breath, movement, divination reflection, and spiritual discipline.

16 Modules One sacred posture for each major Odu.
5 Odu Families Alafia, Etawa, Ejife, Okana, and Oyeku.
1 Living Oracle The body becomes a sacred text in motion.
?

Course Premise

Odu Ara means the body becomes the living expression of Odu. Each Odu is trained as a posture, breath pattern, movement principle, spiritual discipline, and divination reflection.

The student does not only study Odu. The student learns to stand inside the meaning of Odu. The body becomes a divination tray. The breath becomes the offering. The posture becomes the Odu. The movement becomes the prayer.

The 16 SGI Modules

1

Ogbe

Posture of Uplifted Light
The divine that uplifts, supports, and elevates.
Body PrincipleStand tall, chest open, spine lifted, palms receptive.
Training LessonLet your posture raise the spirit before movement begins.
“I am uplifted. I am supported. I rise in divine alignment.”
2

Oyeku

Posture of Sacred Descent
The divine one associated with death, mystery, and the unseen.
Body PrincipleLower the body, fold inward, listen deeply.
Training LessonDescent is not defeat; it is the doorway to hidden power.
“I enter the mystery. I honor the unseen. I return renewed.”
3

Iwori

Posture of Seeking Ori
The divine one who searches for his head.
Body PrincipleOne hand near the head, one near the heart, body centered.
Training LessonSeek alignment with Ori before acting.
“I seek my Ori. I align my head, heart, and destiny.”
4

Edi

Posture of Still Reasoning
Strives for stillness and deep reasoning.
Body PrincipleRoot the feet, steady the breath, quiet the hands.
Training LessonStillness allows wisdom to speak.
“I become still. I reason deeply. I listen before I move.”
5

Irosun

Posture of Steadfast Thought
Holds steadfastly to thought.
Body PrincipleFirm stance, stable core, unwavering gaze.
Training LessonDiscipline begins with the ability to hold the mind steady.
“My thought is steady. My body is firm. My spirit does not scatter.”
6

Oworin

Posture of the Walking Sun
Sets as the sun when walking.
Body PrincipleMove with rhythm, circularity, warmth, and controlled descent.
Training LessonWalk with radiant purpose and graceful transition.
“I walk with the sun. I move with rhythm, warmth, and purpose.”
7

Obara

Posture of Bodily Dominion
Governs the body.
Body PrincipleUpright torso, disciplined limbs, embodied command.
Training LessonGovern the body before attempting to govern the world.
“I govern my body. I command my movement. I stand in discipline.”
8

Okanran

Posture of the Ignited Heart
Ignites the hearts of hearts.
Body PrincipleOpen the chest, awaken inner fire, project presence.
Training LessonLet sacred fire awaken courage, truth, and conviction.
“My heart is ignited. My courage is awake. My truth has fire.”
9

Ogunda

Posture of Sacred Suppression
The divine one suppressing evil.
Body PrincipleAngled stance, cutting arms, focused forward intention.
Training LessonClear obstruction with discipline and righteous force.
“I suppress evil. I cut through obstruction. I clear the path.”
10

Osa

Posture of Seasonal Turning
Who brings about seasons.
Body PrincipleSpiral, turn, shift, and adapt.
Training LessonChange is sacred when it follows divine timing.
“I turn with the season. I change with wisdom. I move in time.”
11

Ika

Posture of Sudden Reflection
Moves quickly by way of trickery and reflection.
Body PrincipleCoiled body, alert eyes, guarded hands, sudden release.
Training LessonBe watchful, strategic, and disciplined with hidden force.
“I move with strategy. I reflect before release. My force is governed.”
12

Oturupon

Posture of Released Force
Who releases.
Body PrincipleDrop the weight, loosen the joints, release tension through the ground.
Training LessonWhat is bound must be released with wisdom.
“I release what is bound. I loosen what is tense. I return to flow.”
13

Otura

Posture of Uncovering Depth
Who digs up or uncovers pits.
Body PrincipleLower awareness, searching hands, investigative movement.
Training LessonReveal what is hidden before stepping forward.
“I uncover what is hidden. I see the pit before I step.”
14

Irete

Posture of Spread Blessing
Who spreads blessing and comfortable goodness.
Body PrincipleBroad stance, open arms, outward-moving hands.
Training LessonAlignment should distribute blessing into the world.
“I spread blessing. I offer goodness. I make comfort visible.”
15

Oshe

Posture of Fulfilled Accomplishment
Brings about accomplishments and fulfillments.
Body PrincipleBalanced finish, steady breath, graceful completion.
Training LessonFulfillment comes when movement, intention, and discipline agree.
“I accomplish. I fulfill. I complete with grace.”
16

Ofun

Posture of Enlightened Completion
The one who is enlightened.
Body PrincipleExpanded body, lifted head, purified presence.
Training LessonEnlightenment is embodied clarity.
“I am enlightened. I am clear. I complete the circle in wisdom.”

Weekly Practice Format

1. Opening

Ground the body, acknowledge Ori, ancestors, and divine wisdom.

2. Odu Meaning

Study the meaning of the Odu and speak its posture name aloud.

3. Body Posture

Enter the posture slowly with breath, alignment, and awareness.

4. Movement Drill

Allow the posture to become stepping, turning, guarding, releasing, or opening.

5. Breath Pattern

Inhale to receive, pause to listen, exhale to express, return to center.

6. Divination Reflection

Ask: What is this Odu teaching my body today?

7. Journal

Record physical sensation, emotional response, spiritual insight, and life application.

8. Closing

Close with the affirmation and return to stillness.

Closing Course Statement

Odu Ara: The 16 Sacred Body Postures teaches that divination is not only read with the eyes or interpreted with the mind. It can also be embodied through posture, breath, discipline, movement, and sacred reflection.

The initiate learns to stand in Odu, move through Odu, breathe with Odu, and live as a conscious vessel of divine alignment.
